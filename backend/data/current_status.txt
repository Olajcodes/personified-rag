# Title: An Automatic Win: My First Week Journey into AI with the TIIDELab Scholarship
"The harder I work, the luckier I get." - Samuel Goldwyn
Acceptance mailThe world of artificial intelligence has always intrigued me, but this past week, I took my first significant step into this fascinating domain through the TIIDELab AI Cohort 6 Fellowship. This unique opportunity, boosted by an automatic scholarship I received through the pre-fellowship program, has already proven to be a transformative experience.
From foundational Python programming to understanding the intricacies of machine learning and fine-tuning AI models, Week 1 has set an exciting tone for the journey ahead. Here's how it all unfolded:

---

Onboarding & Standups: Setting the Tone for Success
Onboarding ImageWe were warmly welcomed by Cynthia (Izuagie) Shobayo, the Program and Partnership Director at TIIDELab, and heard inspiring words from Kadir Salami, Founder of TIIDELab. We also met the amazing team members, including:
Gbotemi Adebayo (Communication Associate)
Aliu Increase (Programs Administrator)
Chibuzo Success Godswill (Task Master)
Emilefo Grace (Volunteer)

TIIDELab TeamTo top it off, Mr. Daniel Wilson, Lead Facilitator and Founder of XRI Global, gave us a taste of what the AI journey would involve. And let's not forget Mr. Aderoju Shamsudeen Adedapo, aka Mr. Shams, who shared invaluable insights from his experience as the immediate past Program Manager of TIIDELab. The onboarding session left me feeling excited and ready for the road ahead.
Every day of the fellowship begins with a morning standup (scrum) at 10 AM, where we review the previous day's class, discuss key insights, and align on goals for the day ahead. These sessions have not only kept us on track but have also fostered a supportive and collaborative learning environment. We discuss our progress, address challenges, and dive deep into class reviews; a great way to start the day energized and focused.
In addition, TIIDELab goes the extra mile by providing each fellow with a 30GB data subscription, ensuring that we have the necessary resources to fully engage in our learning journey. This thoughtful provision underscores TIIDELab's commitment to removing barriers to success and makes them stand out as an organization truly dedicated to the growth and development of its participants.
Day 2: Diving into Python Programming with Mr. Ibrahim Tijani
The first official class on Python programming was nothing short of exhilarating! Mr. Ibrahim Tijani guided us through Python fundamentals, emphasizing how Python serves as the backbone of many AI systems. We explored:
Data Types: Learning how to manipulate and work with numbers, strings, and lists was a real eye-opener.
Control Structures: From basic "if else" statements to loops, the hands-on practice really brought the concepts to life.
Running Python Programs: There is something incredibly rewarding about seeing your code run successfully for the first time!

Introduction to PythonDay 3: Machine Learning (ML) Foundations with Daniel Wilson
On Day 3, Mr. Daniel Wilson took us into the world of machine learning, providing a comprehensive overview of supervised, unsupervised, and semi-supervised learning. One of the key takeaways was the importance of aligning AI-driven solutions with real customer problems. This discussion was especially insightful, as we examined whether to create "vitamin" products (enhancements to existing systems) or "painkiller" products (solutions to urgent issues).
We also explored the implications of ChatGPT's rapid adoption and its cultural significance in enabling personalized AI experiences across diverse languages. A notable highlight was our deep dive into low-resource languages and their potential as blue ocean opportunities in the AI landscape.
Introduction to MLDay 4: Learning from Jonathan Shaw - The Power of Machine Learning
By Day 4, we were already knee-deep in machine learning. Jonathan Shaw broke down key concepts like neural networks, which mimic the human brain, and introduced us to the types of learning algorithms, including:
Supervised Learning: Training models using labeled data.
Unsupervised Learning: Allowing the model to identify patterns on its own.
Self-Supervised & Reinforcement Learning: These advanced approaches push machine learning boundaries, helping models learn from their actions and adjust in real-time.

We also explored essential technical concepts like tokenization and the QKV (Query, Key, Value) mechanism, used to process large datasets. The theory, coupled with practical applications, helped connect the dots for me as I began to see how machine learning is applied in everyday tech solutions.
Day 5: Fine-Tuning AI Models with Dillon Mee & Timothy Khouw
On Day 5, things got even more exciting. Dillon Mee and Timothy Khouw led us through a session on Fine-Tuning AI Models - the process of tailoring pre-trained models to improve performance for specific tasks or datasets. We explored:
Full Fine-Tuning: Adjusting the entire model to adapt to a new task.
LoRA Fine-Tuning: A resource-efficient approach that targets specific layers of a model.
QLoRA Fine-Tuning: Utilizing quantized weights for faster processing without sacrificing performance.

This session was particularly exciting because we had the opportunity to fine-tune a Llama 3.1 8B base model using a small English dataset, allowing us to apply our newfound knowledge to a hands-on project.
Additionally, we were introduced to HuggingFace, a platform full of resources for model training, fine-tuning, and deployment. HuggingFace is now my go-to for understanding how large AI models are implemented and fine-tuned for specific applications.
In this session, we also split into two breakout rooms:
Non-Technical Room: Led by Dillon Mee, focusing on broader AI implementation aspects using Monster API.
Technical Room: Led by Timothy Khouw, where I dove deep into the hands-on process of fine-tuning the Llama model with step-by-step guidance.

---

Reflecting on Week 1: The Journey So Far
As I wrap up Week 1, I am amazed at how much I have learned in such a short amount of time. From foundational Python skills to advanced concepts like neural networks and fine-tuning, the learning curve has been steep but incredibly rewarding. The morning standups, led by miss Increase Aliu, have been a perfect way to start each day with purpose and clarity, ensuring we are aligned and motivated.
Though there have been moments where the material felt overwhelming (hello, neural networks and QKV!), the satisfaction of working through these challenges has been worth it. I am super excited to dive deeper into fine-tuning and take full advantage of platforms like HuggingFace in future projects.
The coming weeks promise even more learning and growth, and I am ready to keep pushing myself as I explore the limitless potential of AI. Stay tuned for more updates as I continue this incredible journey!

# Title: One Month at TIIDELab: Building Skills, Leadership, and AI for Africa
"Tech leadership isn't just about skills; it's about vision, adaptability, and impacting the communities we serve."

---

Joining TIIDELab has been a journey filled with excitement and challenges. This first month has brought invaluable lessons, fresh perspectives, and meaningful connections with mentors and my peers. As the Team Lead for my group, Team DataMinds, in the technical team, I've found myself a learner and a guide, helping our team explore and prune datasets in an African context.
This article will walk you through each week's highlights, capturing the growth, challenges, and breakthroughs that have defined my journey so far. It's a story of building confidence, sharpening skills, and understanding how my work fits into the wider AI and tech landscape.

---

Week 1: Emotional Intelligence and Self-Discovery
Our journey began with a deep dive into Emotional Intelligence and Self-Discovery. I had heard of emotional intelligence but never really explored it as a skill that could be cultivated. Through guided exercises, we reflected on our strengths, weaknesses, and core motivations.
"Self-awareness is a vital part of building a strong foundation in tech and life. Knowing myself better has allowed me to lead my team with empathy and resilience, especially when facing challenges."
The session taught me that understanding my emotions and how to manage them effectively is essential for growth in tech, where challenges and complex problems are the norm. As a team lead, it's now easier for me to connect with my team on a deeper level, staying grounded and motivated.
Ideation and Idea Generation
Later that day, Mrs. Cynthia led us through a liberating session on Ideation and Idea Generation. We were encouraged to step outside our usual thought patterns and brainstorm freely. Mrs. Cynthia emphasized that even the simplest ideas could evolve into something groundbreaking with the right mindset.
"Innovation often comes from the willingness to play with ideas, even the wild ones."
This was more than just a brainstorming exercise; it was about refining ideas and nurturing them into practical solutions. I left feeling empowered to bring my perspective to our projects and encourage my team to do the same.
Week 2: Tokenization
Our first dive into the technical side of AI began with Tokenization, led by Mr. Dillon. It was challenging at first, especially as I took on the responsibility of helping my team understand the core principles of breaking down language into smaller units, or "tokens," which are the building blocks of AI's language comprehension.
"Tokenization felt like a journey into AI's brain - understanding how it reads language and begins to comprehend meaning."
Mr. Dillon's approachable teaching style helped demystify this process. I walked away feeling more prepared and capable, no longer seeing AI as an intimidating realm but something I could engage with and lead my team in exploring.

---

Week 3: Breaking into Emerging Tech Roles and Types of Finetuning
On October 4, Mr. Arome Ibrahim led a session on positioning oneself for success in emerging tech roles. This was an eye-opener, as he broke down the importance of building networks, finding the right niche, and presenting our skills effectively. As a team lead, these insights were invaluable for understanding how I could better support my team's career aspirations as well.
"This session broadened my perspective, making me realize that the tech industry offers countless paths, each one a chance to make a unique impact."
For the first time, I began to see the bigger picture and the different roles that I could grow into, as well as ways to prepare my team to succeed in this fast-paced industry.
Later that week, Mr. Daniel introduced us to Types of Fine-Tuning. Being in the technical team, I initially thought this would involve advanced coding. However, we focused on practical applications, like Data Pruning, which was essential for making AI models perform better by using cleaner, more relevant data.
As a team, Team DataMinds, we tackled the challenging task of pruning a massive dataset of nearly 10,000 entries, tailoring it for African contexts. Leading this task was not only technical but also deeply meaningful. We were making AI relevant to Africa by ensuring that the data represented local realities.
"Pruning the dataset was challenging but rewarding; it's where I truly saw AI's potential to serve and reflect African contexts."

---

Week 4: The Lean Canvas
Our Lean Canvas session on October 9 introduced us to a structured way of organizing ideas. The Lean Canvas is a one-page business model that distills the core elements of a project into clear, actionable components. This was a pivotal tool, helping me guide my team in structuring our project ideas effectively.
"It's about seeing the big picture - understanding the problem, our audience, and what makes our approach unique."
This session taught us that clarity is essential, especially in collaborative projects, where everyone needs to understand both the 'why' and the 'how.'
Week 4 (Later): Product Development
Building on the Lean Canvas session, Product Development emphasized how to turn ideas into reality. Mrs. Cynthia stressed customer segmentation and designing with the user in mind, encouraging us to see our projects from the perspective of the end-user.
"Product development is about empathy - it's about creating something genuinely useful by keeping the user's needs at the center."
This session inspired me to foster an empathetic approach within my team, focusing not just on creating something innovative but something impactful for those who would use it.

---

Evaluation and Validation
Mr. Dillon introduced us to evaluation techniques, showing us how to measure our models' performance. He explained different metrics that check a model's accuracy, reliability, and efficiency. This session underscored the importance of continuous improvement.
"Building a model isn't the end; it's the constant refinement that ensures it remains effective and relevant."
Learning about these metrics highlighted the responsibility we have to make tech trustworthy and to maintain that trust through rigorous testing.

---

Feedback Loops
Mr. Femi's session on Feedback Loops showed us how feedback is crucial to refining any product. A feedback loop keeps us aligned with user needs, ensuring our work remains relevant and functional.
"A good feedback loop isn't about perfection from the start; it's about growing with every iteration."
Understanding the importance of feedback has shifted my approach as a leader, making me more receptive to insights from team members and external sources alike.

---

Communication and Problem Solving
Collaboration is inevitable in tech, and a session on Communication and Problem Solving with Toingrick showed us just how crucial clear, direct communication is, especially in challenging projects. We explored brainstorming techniques and practical ways to tackle complex issues.
"Tech isn't just about coding; it's about working with people, and sometimes, that's where the real challenge lies."
This session reaffirmed the importance of effective communication within Team DataMinds, where we work closely and rely on each other's strengths to overcome challenges.

---

Fireside Chat and Career Goal-Setting
During our week off, we enjoyed a Fireside Chat with a mentor. It was an informal session, giving us the space to ask questions and gain insight from someone seasoned in the industry. It was a grounding reminder that everyone, no matter how accomplished, has had setbacks and learning moments.
Our Career Goal Setting session with Mr. Emmanuel Ihim rounded off the month. He led us through exercises in mapping out personal and professional goals, emphasizing a "start with WHY" approach. His insights on crafting a CV, setting SMART goals, and conducting SWOT analysis were practical and invaluable.
"Purpose-driven career planning is the key to sustainable success. Start with WHY, and the path forward becomes clearer."

---

Daily Standup Meetings and a commitment to Learning
Every day begins with a morning standup meeting at 10 AM. These sessions have been essential for staying organized, motivated, and on track, thanks to the support from Miss Increase Aliu and our coordinator. Discussing our progress, addressing challenges, and reflecting on the previous day's lessons have created a collaborative learning environment that fosters growth.
Additionally, TIIDELab's provision of a 30GB data subscription has been a thoughtful touch, ensuring we have the resources needed to fully engage with the program and stay connected. This level of support sets TIIDELab apart, showing a deep commitment to each fellow's success.

---

Final Reflections: One Month In
This month at TIIDELab has given me not just skills but a stronger sense of purpose and connection. Leading Team DataMinds has allowed me to see the impact that tech and AI can have in shaping Africa's future. I'm excited to continue this journey, embracing new challenges and honing my ability to contribute meaningfully to the tech world.
TIIDELab is not just a program; it's a platform for transformation, equipping us with the tools, insights, and confidence to navigate the future of tech.
